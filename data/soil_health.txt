Soil pH determines nutrient availability to plants.

pH below 6.5 indicates acidic soil.
pH between 6.5 and 7.5 is generally ideal for most crops.
pH above 7.5 indicates alkaline soil.

Nitrogen deficiency causes yellowing of leaves and slow growth.

Phosphorus deficiency causes poor root development and delayed maturity.

Potassium deficiency causes weak stems and reduced disease resistance.

Organic matter improves soil fertility, water retention, and microbial activity.

Soil testing should be performed regularly to monitor nutrient levels and soil health.
